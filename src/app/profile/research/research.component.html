Research Focus ​ Broadly, my research addresses how lower status and higher status group members' beliefs, attitudes, and behaviors uphold systems of oppression. Likewise, I am interested in people's resistance to hierarchy, examining relevant predictors and processes. Specifically, my passion lies in understanding sexism. Besides the research conducted for my theses, I have led various research projects on sexism-related topics. For instance, in another stream of research, I examine when and why men and women oppose abortion. I also conduct research examining prejudice against gender non-conforming people, including personal and country-level predictors of opposition toward people with diverse genders and sexualities. Further, I study gender differences in sexual objectification processes and the impact of media (e.g., video games, advertising, pornography) on men and women's attitudes and behaviors toward women. As part of a broader social justice focus, I have also shaped interventions to mitigate the climate change emergency. ​ Ultimately, my goal is to understand intergroup relations and use social psychological theories and empirical findings to test interventions that can reduce prejudice and discrimination. In this way, I use theory and data to solve real-world problems related to social justice. Research Skills and Practices ​ My research spans cross-sectional, experimental, longitudinal, and multilevel contexts. In these contexts, I have expertise in research conceptualization, study design, survey construction, data collection (online and in-person), data cleaning, data analysis and interpretation, and reporting/visualizing results. I am proficient with SPSS, AMOS, Mplus, LIWC, Qualtrics, Amazon Mechanical Turk, and CloudResearch. I have experience with R and HLM. I am a peer reviewer for research manuscripts submitted to feminist and other social psychology journals, including Psychology of Women Quarterly and Sex Roles. ​ I value transparency and Open Science. As a strong proponent of preregistration, data-sharing, and collaboration, I regularly engage in these practices.
